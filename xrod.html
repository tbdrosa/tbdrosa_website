Test Shoot

Testing Out New Camera with Elijah Rodriguez

10.13.2020


~4 min read

With my buddy and fellow photographer Elijah Rodriguez. This shoot was probably the week of March 15, 2020. I distinctly remember it being a Thursday right before the United States and the rest of the world shut down. It had to have been a Thursday because I had a heavy Calculus exam the following week and many of my classmates, along with myself, were eager for the exam to be pushed back.. Little did we know what would soon come to pass...

That same day – after I arrived home from class the camera I had purchased and ordered off Depop about a month prior finally shipped and arrived at my house.

My mother, of course, gave me about three windex-wipes to bathe in disinfectant. It was still the early phases of the Covid Shutdown. Not many of us regular civilans were fully aware of the severity of the situation – nor did we fully understand how the virus seemed to travel. I remember an array of conspiracy theories it lasts up to several weeks to months on a surface with no way of being able to get clean it away. Of course, this was largely due to the media trying to underwhelm and not cause massive panic along with the lack of leadership we had in the White House. While all of this was going on in the "background" I texted one of my oldest friends, Elijah, if he had wanted to go on a test shoot since I had just come into contact with my new camera and had no way of knowing if the camera actually worked. I was in need of a model and he was surprisingly free. It's often the case whenever I'm free my friends are super busy – or working. We met underneath the One Train platoform on 242nd and decided to walk through Van Courtland aimlessly.
