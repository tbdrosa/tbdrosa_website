Dorm Room Shoot - Summit 203

11.07.2020


~2 min read

Dear friend,

I am writing this after the fantastic results on this historic day. Was inspired to write a mini blog post - for my fellow photographers or artists in general, have you ever created a piece of art and had fun doing it, but afterward, when you looked over your work, you hated it all? I, too, have been there. And the best thing I can say is to put it away, somewhere safe that it won't get lost or damaged. Leave it alone for a while. Don't touch it. Please, do not come back to it until you forget that you even made it. I am not sure if this some revolutionary advice; however, it is something that I have been doing for the better part of a year now. For any shoot that I have done for free, I often take a long time with my photo editing because I enjoy returning to my images once I have forgotten them. Usually, when I first take a series of images from a shoot, I am overly critical of them if they are brand new. However, once I return to them once I have forgotten, they are far better than I initially thought. Unfortunately, for those painters, you cannot edit your painting or drawings in the same manner - but I feel the principal could still apply. You may hate your piece, but come to love it later on. I hope this inspires you to create something, forget about it so that you can fall in love with them all over again.

The following series of images are from a while ago. I hope you enjoy them just as much as I did while editing them.
